Overview

Two disciplines, one method.

Pharmacy trained me to trust nothing until it's verified — every dose checked, every interaction cross-referenced, every result documented. Data analysis asks for the same instinct, just pointed at spreadsheets instead of prescriptions.

At Seven-Up Bottling Company, I applied that instinct to operational data: cleaning inconsistent records, standardizing formats, and building the reports supervisors relied on. I later founded my university's first student-led bioinformatics team, training 500+ students in Excel, SQL, Python, and Power BI — which meant translating technical logic into plain, written documentation a beginner could follow without a meeting.

That combination — clinical precision plus a teacher's instinct for clarity — is what I bring to a data team.
